# Regional Investment Allocation: Executive Overview

[cite_start]This site summarizes the feasibility analysis conducted for the **ConnectGreaterWashington** Board[cite: 1]. [cite_start]The study evaluates three development sites to identify their financial profiles through metrics such as **Residual Land Value (RLV)** and **stress-test resilience**[cite: 6, 7].

### Portfolio Candidates
* [cite_start]**Project Connect (North Bethesda, MD):** A compact, transit-oriented life science hub[cite: 8, 11].
* [cite_start]**Tysons Central (Tysons, VA):** An adaptive reuse project transforming aging office infrastructure[cite: 27, 29].
* [cite_start]**The Sanctuary at Poplar Point (Ward 8, DC):** A waterfront redevelopment focused on intergenerational wellness and ecological restoration[cite: 46, 87].

### Core Evaluation Objectives
The goal of this allocation is to translate feasibility math into public communication, prioritizing:
1.  [cite_start]**Economic Resilience:** Stress-testing project viability against market volatility[cite: 7, 16].
2.  [cite_start]**Policy Leverage:** Aligning private development with jurisdictional mandates[cite: 66, 67].
3.  [cite_start]**Equitable Growth:** Addressing historic underinvestment and critical service gaps[cite: 84].
