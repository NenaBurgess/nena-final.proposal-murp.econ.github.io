# Regional Investment Allocation: Executive Overview

This site summarizes the feasibility analysis conducted for the ConnectGreaterWashington Board. The study evaluates three development sites to identify their financial profiles through metrics such as Residual Land Value (RLV) and stress-test resilience.

### Portfolio Candidates
* **Project Connect (North Bethesda, MD):** A compact, transit-oriented life science hub.
* **Tysons Central (Tysons, VA):** An adaptive reuse project transforming aging office infrastructure.
* **The Sanctuary at Poplar Point (Ward 8, DC):** A waterfront redevelopment focused on intergenerational wellness and ecological restoration.

### Core Evaluation Objectives
The goal of this allocation is to translate feasibility math into public communication, prioritizing:
1. **Economic Resilience:** Stress-testing project viability against market volatility.
2. **Policy Leverage:** Aligning private development with jurisdictional mandates.
3. **Equitable Growth:** Addressing historic underinvestment and critical service gaps.
