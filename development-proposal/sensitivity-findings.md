# Sensitivity Findings & Risk Interpretation

All three sites were subjected to three stress scenarios: a rent decrease, a hard cost increase, and a cap rate shock.

### Economic Resilience Ranking
1. **High Resilience (Project Connect):** This site proved the most resilient, passing all three stress tests. Its stability is driven by high-credit, long-term institutional leases from partners like NIH and the University of Maryland.
2. **Moderate Resilience (Tysons Central):** This project narrowly passes two of three scenarios but fails under cap rate adjustments. Office cap rates in Tysons have expanded to 7.5%–8.0%, reflecting high operational risks associated with hybrid-work trends.
3. **Low Resilience (Poplar Point):** This site does not survive sensitivity tests for construction cost increases or rent delays. In a -10% Rent Stress Case, Poplar Point's RLV drops to a negative ($3.9M), breaking feasibility.

### Interpretation of Risk
Poplar Point’s financial volatility is characteristic of high-impact investments at this scale. Its long-term viability depends on early-stage public subsidies and gap financing to offset substantial upfront risks.
