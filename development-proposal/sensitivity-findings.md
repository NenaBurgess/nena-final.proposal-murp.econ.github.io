# Sensitivity Findings & Risk Interpretation

[cite_start]All three sites were subjected to three stress scenarios: a rent decrease, a hard cost increase, and a cap rate shock[cite: 139].

### Economic Resilience Ranking
1.  [cite_start]**High Resilience (Project Connect):** This site proved the most resilient, passing all three stress tests[cite: 17, 137]. [cite_start]Its stability is driven by high-credit, long-term institutional leases from partners like NIH and the University of Maryland[cite: 13, 18].
2.  [cite_start]**Moderate Resilience (Tysons Central):** This project narrowly passes two of three scenarios but fails under cap rate adjustments[cite: 35]. [cite_start]Office cap rates in Tysons have expanded to 7.5%–8.0%, reflecting high operational risks associated with hybrid-work trends[cite: 39].
3.  [cite_start]**Low Resilience (Poplar Point):** This site does not survive sensitivity tests for construction cost increases or rent delays[cite: 54]. [cite_start]In a **-10% Rent Stress Case**, Poplar Point's RLV drops to a negative **($3.9M)**, breaking feasibility[cite: 386, 394].

### Interpretation of Risk
[cite_start]Poplar Point’s financial volatility is characteristic of high-impact investments at this scale[cite: 55]. [cite_start]Its long-term viability depends on early-stage public subsidies and gap financing to offset substantial upfront risks[cite: 56, 396].
