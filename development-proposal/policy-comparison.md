# Policy Comparison: Strategic Alignment

[cite_start]Policy leverage is defined as the alignment of a project with public goals to justify public financing and civic value[cite: 66].

### Alignment Summary
* [cite_start]**Poplar Point (High Leverage):** This project transforms 110 acres by embedding the District’s waterfront wellness vision into the specific needs of Ward 8[cite: 68]. [cite_start]It addresses healthcare and nutrition gaps for a neighborhood where residents currently face 45-minute commutes for fresh food[cite: 69, 79].
* [cite_start]**Project Connect (Moderate Strength):** While supported by a $10M state life-science grant, its narrow focus primarily benefits a high-wealth renter profile and fails to address broader regional spatial inequities[cite: 15, 71, 72].
* [cite_start]**Tysons Central (Weak Alignment):** Primarily remains reliant on private-market performance rather than proactive policy intervention[cite: 74]. [cite_start]It lacks direct financial gap-funding[cite: 73].

### Public Financing Potential
[cite_start]The Sanctuary at Poplar Point leverages **$110M from the FY 2027 budget**, alongside the Housing Production Trust Fund and Supermarket Tax Incentives, to deliver a high return on every public dollar[cite: 68, 80].
