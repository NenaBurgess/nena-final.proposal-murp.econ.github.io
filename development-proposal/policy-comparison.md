# Policy Comparison: Strategic Alignment

Policy leverage is defined as the alignment of a project with public goals to justify public financing and civic value.

### Alignment Summary
* **Poplar Point (High Leverage):** This project transforms 110 acres by embedding the District’s waterfront wellness vision into the specific needs of Ward 8. It addresses healthcare and nutrition gaps for a neighborhood where residents currently face 45-minute commutes for fresh food.
* **Project Connect (Moderate Strength):** While supported by a $10M state life-science grant, its narrow focus primarily benefits a high-wealth renter profile and fails to address broader regional spatial inequities.
* **Tysons Central (Weak Alignment):** Primarily remains reliant on private-market performance rather than proactive policy intervention. It lacks direct financial gap-funding.

### Public Financing Potential
The Sanctuary at Poplar Point leverages $110M from the FY 2027 budget, alongside the Housing Production Trust Fund and Supermarket Tax Incentives, to deliver a high return on every public dollar.
